*30 May 2027*

Well, it's finally happening. After more spreadsheets than any holiday has a
right to demand we are, at last, Europe-bound.

This is a test entry. Its job is to prove the new Europe blog folder, the
template, and the publishing pipeline all hang together before the real
chapters start. If you're reading this on the live site with a sensible title,
a date line, and working navigation, then everything downstream is wired
correctly and you can delete this post with a clear conscience.

A few things this entry is quietly checking:

- The chapter number (`1`) becomes the page title prefix and sets the reading
  order: **"1: The Best-Laid Plans of Mice and Knights"**.
- The *italic date* sits neatly under the title.
- With no `prev:` or `next:` set, the footer nav should read
  "← Back to contents · Back to contents" — exactly what a lone first chapter
  should do.
- An [external link](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grand_Tour) renders as a
  normal Markdown link, while a wikilink to a not-yet-written chapter like
  Roman Holiday gracefully degrades to plain text instead of a broken
  link.

Next stop: Rome. Assuming the flights, the trains, and our knees all cooperate.
